Our Story & Mission

Why Dr. Laurie does what she does.

A mother's conviction became a mission.

"I had been watching these clinics for years — handing out narcotics like they were the only tool in the box, or rushing patients straight to surgery without ever asking why. That's not healing. That's not medicine the way I believe it should be practiced."

Lasting Results
Our goal isn't to keep you coming back — it's to help you feel so well, you don't have to.

Dr. Laurie didn't arrive at regenerative medicine by accident. She was pushed there by something far more powerful than a career decision — her sons.

Her oldest son served as an infantry Marine in the United States Marine Corps, completing two tours of duty. The demands of that service took a significant toll on his body. When he returned, Dr. Laurie watched the VA work through his injuries. What she saw troubled her deeply: a system too quick to recommend surgery, and providers too willing to reach for narcotics and steroid injections as the first and only answer to pain.

Her younger son was a competitive mixed martial artist — and with that came a different set of physical challenges, but the same frustrating pattern when he sought care. Quick diagnoses. Fast referrals to surgery. Prescriptions to numb the pain rather than find its source.

Dr. Laurie and her partners founded In Good Health in 2006 with a different vision — a family practice built around something most clinics had stopped offering: time, attention, and a genuine commitment to understanding what was actually happening in a patient's body.

Today, Dr. Laurie takes that mission a step further by bringing care directly to her patients across Tennessee and Georgia — private consultations, regenerative therapies, and follow-up care — so her patients never have to compromise on quality again.

Frequently Asked

Questions we hear often.

What happens during a consultation with Dr. Davis?
Your consultation is a real, one-on-one conversation with Dr. Davis herself — via Google Meet. She'll review your health history, discuss your symptoms and goals, and help determine which therapies may be right for you. No gatekeepers, no screeners — just Dr. Davis.
Do I need to come in person?
Initial consultations are conducted via Google Meet, making it simple to connect from anywhere in Tennessee or Georgia. Some treatments may require in-person visits. Dr. Davis also offers select in-home care services for qualifying patients.
What if I'm not sure if I qualify?
That's exactly what the consultation is for. Dr. Davis has worked with patients across a wide spectrum of health concerns. The best first step is simply a conversation — she'll give you an honest assessment of your options.
Are these treatments covered by insurance?
Many of Dr. Davis's advanced therapies operate outside traditional insurance models — which is precisely what allows her to offer truly personalized, unrestricted care. She's happy to discuss investment and options during your consultation.
How is stem cell therapy different from other treatments?
Rather than suppressing symptoms, stem cell therapy supports your body's own repair processes at the cellular level. It's regenerative by nature — designed to create lasting improvement, not ongoing dependency on medication.
What states does Dr. Davis serve?
Dr. Davis is licensed in both Tennessee and Georgia. Patients across both states can access virtual consultations, with in-person and select in-home services available in the greater Chattanooga area.
