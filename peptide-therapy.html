Understanding Peptides

Amino acid chains that
speak your body’s language

Peptides are short chains of amino acids — the building blocks of proteins — that act as biological signals throughout your body. They tell your cells to burn fat, rebuild tissue, regulate inflammation, produce hormones, and repair damage.

Your body naturally produces hundreds of peptides. But age, stress, poor sleep, chronic inflammation, and environmental factors reduce their production over time. The result: slower recovery, increased body fat, declining energy, poor sleep, weakened immunity, and accelerated aging.

“Peptide therapy isn’t about adding something foreign to your body — it’s about restoring the signals your body already knows how to use.”

Dr. Laurie uses targeted peptide protocols — customized to your goals and health profile — to help patients achieve results that diet, exercise, and conventional medicine often can’t deliver alone.
