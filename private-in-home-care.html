Why In-Home Care Is Different

The clinic was never
built for you

The modern medical clinic was designed around efficiency — not patient experience. High patient volume, short appointments, standardized protocols, and insurance billing requirements have reduced the average primary care visit to under 15 minutes of actual physician time.

In that environment, your provider cannot possibly understand your life, your patterns, your history, or your goals. They can only react to the immediate complaint — and move on to the next patient.

“When I come to your home, I can see how you live. That changes everything about how I understand your health.” — Dr. Laurie

Private in-home care removes every one of these constraints. Dr. Laurie schedules time to give you real attention. She comes to your home — where you are comfortable, where she can observe your environment, and where the clinical noise that distorts so many medical visits simply doesn’t exist.

A Word About Investment
This is private medicine — designed for those who choose to invest in themselves

Dr. Laurie’s in-home care is not covered by insurance — by design. Insurance reimbursement limits the time, the therapies, and the depth of care a provider can offer. Removing that constraint is what makes this model possible.

The investment reflects private doctoral-level care, delivered personally, in your home. Patients consistently describe it as among the highest-return decisions they’ve made for their health.

Pricing is never posted publicly because every patient is different. Transparent pricing is always discussed during your initial consultation — before any commitment is made.
